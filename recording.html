This notice explains how Spam Kathy handles the recording of phone calls. It applies to anyone who calls a business phone number that Spam Kathy screens, and to the businesses that use our service.

The short version. Kathy answers inbound calls to a business line. At the start of every call she says she is an automated assistant and that the call may be recorded. If you do not want to be recorded, hang up and contact the business another way. Kathy does not make outbound calls.

1. Who is speaking

Kathy is an automated voice assistant. She is software, not a person. She is not a live employee of the business you called and she does not pretend to be one.

At the beginning of every call, before any conversation takes place, Kathy identifies herself as an automated assistant. If you ask her whether she is a person, she will tell you she is not.

2. Inbound calls only

Spam Kathy does not place outbound calls, cold calls, telemarketing calls, or automated dialing of any kind. Kathy only answers calls that someone else has placed to a business line she screens.

If you receive a call from an automated voice claiming to be Spam Kathy, it did not come from us.

3. What gets recorded

When Kathy answers a call, the following may be recorded or stored:

  • Audio of the call, including anything said by the caller and by Kathy
  • A written transcript of the call
  • The calling number, the time of the call, and how long it lasted
  • Whether Kathy passed the call through or screened it, and why

If Kathy passes a call through to a person at the business, the recording of the portion after the transfer is governed by that business's own practices, not by this notice.

4. Notice and consent

Kathy discloses at the start of every call that the call may be recorded. Continuing the call after that disclosure indicates consent to be recorded.

Recording laws differ by state. Some states require that only one party to a call consent to recording. Others require that everyone on the call consent. Because a caller may be in any state, Kathy discloses recording on every call regardless of where the caller is.

If you do not consent to being recorded, hang up. You can reach the business by other means. Hanging up before or during the disclosure means no conversation is retained beyond the basic call metadata described in section 3.

5. How long we keep recordings

Call recordings and transcripts are retained for [RETENTION PERIOD] and then deleted, unless a longer period is required to resolve a dispute, comply with a legal obligation, or enforce our agreements.

Businesses using Spam Kathy can access their own call recordings and transcripts through their dashboard for as long as they remain a customer.

6. Who can access a recording

  • The business that received the call. They can listen to it and read the transcript.
  • Our staff, when needed to operate, troubleshoot, or improve the service.
  • Our service providers, including the voice AI platform that powers Kathy, under contract and only to provide the service.
  • Law enforcement or a court, where we are legally required to produce them.

We do not sell call recordings. We do not share them with advertisers.

7. Requesting deletion of a recording

If you called a business screened by Kathy and you want the recording of your call deleted, email info@spamkathy.com with the number you called from and the approximate time of the call. We will locate the recording and delete it, subject to any legal obligation to retain it.

We may need to verify that the request comes from the number in question before we act on it.

8. If you are a business using Spam Kathy

You are responsible for your own compliance with call recording law in the states where your callers are located, and for any notice or consent your own team is required to give after a call is passed through to them. Kathy's disclosure covers the portion of the call she handles. It does not cover what happens after she hands the call to your staff.

Why this document needs a lawyer specifically.

Call recording is governed by both federal law and a patchwork of state laws. Texas is a one-party consent state, but your callers will not all be in Texas. California, Florida, Illinois, Pennsylvania, Washington, Massachusetts, Maryland, Michigan, Montana, Nevada, New Hampshire, Oregon, Connecticut, and Delaware have all-party or otherwise stricter rules, and the penalties in several of them are criminal, not just civil.

Separately, Kathy is an AI voice. Several states have enacted or proposed AI disclosure requirements, and the FCC has ruled that AI-generated voices are "artificial" for TCPA purposes. That ruling targets outbound robocalls, which is why "inbound only" appears throughout this notice and why it should stay true. If Spam Kathy ever places outbound calls, this document and your TCPA exposure both change completely.

Get this reviewed by an attorney who does telecom or privacy work, not a general business lawyer.
